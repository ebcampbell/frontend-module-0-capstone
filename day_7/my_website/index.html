Fall Whiskey Sunset

This seasonal drink is a variation on Martha Stewarts Cider-Bourbon Cocktail.

  • 3 oz Whiskey
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 - 1.5 oz pear juice
  • 3/4 inch ginger rough chopped
  • Turbinado sugar
  1. Muddle ginger and sugar togeter in mixer
  2. Add juice, lemon zest, and pear juice
  3. Add whiskey
  4. Shake mixer and pour contents in a glass over ice

Breakfast Egg Fried Rice

Simple fried rice recipe using day old rice.

  • 2 eggs
  • Cooking oil
  • 1 cup day old cooked rice
  • 1 tbsp Tamari (GF soy sauce)
  • 1/2 tbsp seasame oil
  • Bacon or suasage
  1. Beat the eggs in a mixing bowl.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium high heat. Once heated, add some cooking oil. Reduce the heat to medium. Pour in the beaten eggs. When the egg starts cooking, break the egg into smaller pieces using a fork or chopsticks.
  3. Add the rice when the egg is partly (70 – 80%) cooked and stir the rice evenly. Add the soy sauce and gently mix with the rest of ingredients. (Optional) Add some sesame oil, sesame seeds and green onions then gently toss to coat.
  4. Serve

Ancho Chile Flank Steak and Sweet Potato Tacos

For an extra burst of flavor, stir in a few tablespoons of pan drippings into the sour cream.

  • 2 lb. flank steak
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 tsp. garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp. plus one tsp. ground cumin, divided
  • 4 tsp salt divided
  • 1/4 cup plus 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 lb. fresh tomatillos, husks removed
  • 1 1/2 lb. sweet potatos, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 1 large red onion, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • Corn tortillas, sour cream, and cilantro
  1. Place flank steak in a large zip-top plastic freezer bag. Stir together lime juice, next 2 ingredients, 1 Tbsp. cumin, 1 Tbsp. ancho chile powder, and 2 tsp. kosher salt in a small bowl. Whisk in 1/4 cup olive oil, and pour over flank steak. Seal bag, and turn to coat. Chill 1 to 12 hours.
  2. Place oven rack about 6 inches from top of oven. Preheat oven to 450°. Rinse tomatillos, and cut into quarters. Stir together sweet potatoes, red onion, tomatillos, and remaining 1 tsp. cumin, 1 tsp. ancho chile powder, 2 tsp. salt, and 2 Tbsp. oil in a large bowl. Spread sweet potato mixture in an even layer in a heavy-duty aluminum foil-lined sheet pan.
  3. Bake at 450° for 20 minutes. Remove from oven, and move sweet potato mixture to outer edges of pan. Place flank steak in center of pan. Increase oven temperature to broil.
  4. Broil 6 minutes. Turn steak over, and broil 6 minutes more. (Stir vegetables if they begin to char.) Remove from oven, and let stand 5 minutes. Cut steak across the grain, and drizzle with pan drippings. Serve with sweet potato mixture, tortillas, sour cream, and fresh cilantro.

SERVES 6 -8
ACTIVE 30 min
TOTAL 2 hours

Deviled Potatoes

Two Southern classics, eviled eggs and potato salad join forces create this tasty appetizer.

  • 1 lb. petite potatoes
  • 1 tbl olive oil
  • Salt
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbl capers, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tsp chopped parsley
  • 2 tsp chopped fresh dill
  • 2 tsp whole grain mustard
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  1. Preheat oven to 350F. Place potatoes in a small bowl, drizzle with oil. Sprinkle salt; toss to coat. Spread potatoes in a single layer on a bakeing sheet and bake until tender when pierced, about 40 min. Remove from oven and cool.
  2. Cut potatoes in half crosswise. Sccop out potato pulp into bowl, leaving shells intact. Place shells cut side up on baking sheet and bake in preheated oven until dry, about 10 min. Cool completely.
  3. Stir together potato pulp, sour cream, capers, parsley, dill, mustard, zest, and salt. Spoon mixture generously into each shell.

SERVES 10(serving size: 3 peices)
ACTIVE 25 min
TOTAL 2 hours

Grilled Peaches with whipped cream

  • Firm but ripe peaches
  • Sugar
  • vanilla
  • 1/2 cup chilled whipping cream
  1. Prepare barbecue (medium-high heat). Arrange peach halves, cut side up, on baking sheet; sprinkle peaches with sugar. Let stand until sugar begins to soften, about 10 minutes. Place peaches on grill, sugared side down. Grill until tender and slightly charred, about 4 minutes per side. Transfer peach halves to plates.
  2. Using electric mixer, beat whipping cream and vanilla in bowl until peaks form; Spoon whipped cream alongside peaches and serve.
